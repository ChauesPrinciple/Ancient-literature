Learning Objectives

Upon completion of this module, students will be able to:

  1. MO13.1 Interpret the relevance of "Book of Wikked Wyves" and what Chaucer was trying to say about men’s view of women.
  2. MO13.2 Infer the role society plays in framing our reality, and how we view and interact with those around us through the "Book of Wikked Wyves" effect on Jankyn.

  3. MO13.3 Define a person, or character’s, agency, what it means to have agency, and what it does and does not look like to accept another’s right to agency. 

  4. MO13.4 Discuss the role of self-narrative, or the internalizing of societal expectations, on our behavior towards others. 

  5. MO13.5 Review and critique fellow students’ work using the guidelines within the discussion board.   

Learning Activities

  1. Read The Wife of Bath, from the Canterbury Tales, and the accompanying background, videos, and analysis. Answer the question at the end of the text.

  2. This week, please view the following multimedia materials:

    • This video provides a summary and analysis of the Wife's Prologue and Tale.
    1.  Deep Dive: Middle English

      2. Middle English Introduction (Alexander Arguelles)
  3. Complete: This week, we need to complete the Literary Quick Take for this week's story, "The Wife of Bath's Prologue and Tale" from The Canterbury Tales, and we also need to ensure we have posted responses to other students for the Chapter Three Theory Discussion.
