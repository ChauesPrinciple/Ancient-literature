Learning Objectives

Upon completion of this module, students will be able to:

  1. MO5.1 Apply and utilize vocabulary highlighted within the texts (also provided on the flashcards) accurately in written responses. (CO 1, 4)  
  2. MO5.2 Identify key concepts, plot, and characters for the texts we have covered in Chapter One. (CO 1, 2, 3, 4)  
  3. MO5.3 Produce a concise summary of two texts. (CO 1, 2)
  4. MO5.4 Develop critical analysis of a chosen text. (CO 1, 2, 3, 4)
  5. MO5.5 engage with a text from a critical and contemporary standpoint. (CO 1, 2, 3, 4)
  6. MO5.6 Correctly use and cite quotations from a text to support analysis. (CO 4)  
  7. MO5.7 Discuss the implications of belief regarding the placebo effect and Don Quixote’s power of faith. (CO 2)

Learning Activities

  1. Read Don Quixote, as well as the accompanying Background, Summary, Analysis, and corresponding videos. Complete the question at the end of the text.

  2. This week, please view the following multimedia materials:

    • This video is a fun animated summary of major concepts in the text.

    • This video by Thug Notes is a summary, but also a deeper dive into an analysis of the text.
    •  Deep Dive: Satire and Irony
      • What is Irony?
      • A Free World Needs Satire (TED)
  3. Complete: This week marks the end of our first Chapter, so there is a lot to do.
    1. First, as usual, please complete the Literary Quick Take for our reading, Don Quixote. 
    2. On Monday, the formative Chapter One Formative Quiz will be due. This quiz can be taken up to 10 times, and it is meant to be a study guide for the concepts we are expected to use on the End of Chapter Test.
    3. You must score a 90% or higher on the Quiz to take the test! The End of Chapter Test will be due on Friday. Please set aside time, as the test is a written response, and will take longer than the quiz.
