Learning Objectives

Upon completion of this module, students will be able to:

  1. MO10.7 Reflect on the implications of the conflicting moral systems that Hamlet is caught between, and how that affects our reading of the play.
  2. MO10.2 Compare how all three tales: the Mwindo Epic, the Bhagavad Gita, and Hamlet reflect the “Betrayal of What is Right”, within Dr. Shay’s work, and compare how each story views revenge.
  3. MO10.3 apply and utilize vocabulary highlighted within the texts (also provided on the flashcards) accurately in written responses.
  4. MO10.4 Identify key concepts, plot, and characters for the texts we have covered in Chapter Two.
  5. MO10.5 Produce a concise summary of two texts.
  6. MO10.6 Develop a critical analysis of a chosen text
  7. MO10.7 engage with a text from a critical and contemporary standpoint
  8. MO10.8 Correctly use and cite quotations from a text to support an analysis.

  2. Read: Hamlet. Answer the question at the end of the text.

    2. On Monday, the formative Chapter Two Formative Quiz will be due. This quiz can be taken up to 10 times, and it is meant to be a study guide for the concepts we are expected to use on the End of Chapter Test.
    3. You must score a 90% or higher on the Quiz to take the test! The End of Chapter Test will be due on Friday. Please set aside time, as the test is a written response, and will take longer than the quiz.
