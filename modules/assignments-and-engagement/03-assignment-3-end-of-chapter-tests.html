Assignment #3: End of Chapter Tests

Assignment #3 Chapter Tests

Instructions

Purpose

The purpose of this assignment is to demonstrate your knowledge of the text, to demonstrate your ability to use effective evidence, and to demonstrate the ability to properly quote and engage with the text. You will also need to demonstrate critical thinking when crafting a specific and argumentative point about the text.

Criteria

A quality essay response will directly address the prompt, the text, and any critical materials provided in the prompt. It should be well developed with a central thesis.

Your response should follow the structure of a well-developed three-paragraph short essay:

  1. your claim,
  2. your analysis of the text with specific evidence,
  3. your takeaway and the implications of what your analysis means.
  • Length: Three total paragraphs as described below
  • Sources: Use direct evidence as described below
  • Documentation: Correct MLA documentation is expected, including in-text citations and Works Cited. If you include any source material without giving proper credit, you risk either earning a zero on the assignment or an F as your final grade in the course.

Task

To fulfill this task with a quality response, please:

  1. Write three well-formed paragraphs.
    1. The first paragraph should set up, give the necessary background, and end with a specific statement that is a direct answer to the question posed.
      1. First, provide any details or plot points your audience may have forgotten that may be important to your claim.
      2. State a specific claim (the thesis)

An easy way to get a working thesis and make sure you are on track is to turn the question being asked into a statement.

  1. Directly address the question.
    1. Do not make vague or unprovable statements.
    2. Do not simply recapitulate the plot of the text.
    3. Be persuasive.
      1. Ask yourself, would this convince someone who disagrees with me?
  2. Engage with the material.
    1. Use direct quotes to prove your point.
      1. Utilizing quotes within your own sentence structure to prove your point is advanced but very effective.

E.g., We know the wood creations of the Popol Vuh “climbed to the roofs of the houses,” and “tried to mount to the tops of the trees” (1), but ultimately, they all…
