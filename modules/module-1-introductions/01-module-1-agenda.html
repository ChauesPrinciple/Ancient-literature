Module 1 Agenda

Module 1 Agenda

Welcome to Early World Literature! This course is designed as an Open Education and Resource. This means that it is free, with no text costs, and is designed and written by your instructors. You can find your textbook here.

For this week, our goal is to become familiar with the course, its structure, and its purpose. You will be reading the introductions to our text and each of the modules for this class to get a better understanding of the course as a whole. You will also be introducing yourself to your classmates and your instructor. Please check the assignment calendar often to stay up to date on assignments and readings!

This module will cover the following topics/concepts:

  •  Overview of the course, including syllabus, course shell, assignments, and introductions.
  • Examination of the assignments and the differences between formative and summative assessments.
  • The use of theory to frame our readings.

Learning Objectives

When writing learning objectives for a course or module, remember:

  1. MO1.1 Identify “Getting Started” in the course shell, including syllabus, schedule, assignments, and netiquette. 
  2. MO1.2 Examine the major assignments for the course.    
  3. MO1.3 Distinguish the differences between formative and summative assessments.   
  4. MO1.4 Define theory and explain its purpose in literary analysis. (CO 3, 4)
  5. MO1.5 Discuss what you need to engage the course material as a student. 

Learning Activities

  1. Read the following materials: the Getting Started module, the Introduction to the Text, the introductions for chapters One, Two, and Three (skimming these is fine), and the Assignments for the course.

  2. This week, please view the following multimedia materials:

    1. Why We Read

    • This video by Crash Courses explains what we can gain from reading, why it is important, and what we can get out of the process.
    1. Deep Dive: A World History Timeline

    • This video gives a very thorough timeline of history and events in the ancient world. This is not necessary, but will certainly help with keeping all the times and dates accurate in your mind.
    1. Extra Help: Course and Terms
      1. Full Suite of How to Use Brightspace! (Bright Space)
      2. Literary Terms (A Lovely Jaunt)
      3. Reading for Understanding (A Lovely Jaunt)
  3. Complete the following learning activit[y/ies]: Introductions Discussion and Syllabus and Course Quiz.
    • Please take the time to familiarize yourself with the discussion board, the netiquette expected of the class, and introduce yourself to your classmates.

Reflect & Discuss

Engage with the text and your peers using the reading questions above.
