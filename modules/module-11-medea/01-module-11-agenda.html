Learning Objectives

Upon completion of this module, students will be able to:

  1. MO11.1 Examine the role gender plays in societal expectations and what is considered acceptable behavior in relation to both Medea and a contemporary example
  2. MO11.2 Define abjection and identifying examples of behavior that create a sense of abjection
  3. MO11.3 Analyze the role that narrative plays in cementing power dynamics, especially when they are unequal.
  4. MO11.4 Differentiate between violent emotions, violent actions, and the role gender plays in either.
  5. MO11.5 Evaluate peer thesis for competency, clarity, and scope.

Learning Activities

  1. Read the Chapter 3 Introduction.
  2. Read: Historical background, summary, and lecture for Medea. 
  3. Read Medea. Answer the question at the end of the text.

  4. This week, please view the following multimedia materials:

    • This video provides an animated summary of Jason and the Argonauts, which is essential background for understanding Medea.

    • This video provides a summary and analysis of the play Medea, as well as effective background, contextual, and theoretical summaries.
    1.  Deep Dive: Abjection and Gender
      1. Your Behavior Creates Your Gender (Big Think)
      2. Abject Meaning and Abject Examples (I Swear English)
  5. Complete: This week, we need to complete the Literary Quick Take for this week's reading, Medea, and our initial post for the Chapter Three Theory Discussion. We also need to continue our ongoing Peer Work for our Literary Analysis by posting and evaluating each others' thesis. 

Reflect & Discuss

Engage with the text and your peers using the reading questions above.
