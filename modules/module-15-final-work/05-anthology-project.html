Anthology Project

Anthology Project

Purpose

This is a creative and editorial project. You will design your own anthology around a single cohesive theme (just as our modules unify texts). Your role is that of an editor curating texts into a meaningful collection.

Important: Your Literary Analysis essay becomes the anthology’s Foreword. It serves as the critical introduction to the one required class text you are including, demonstrating why it belongs in your anthology and how it connects to your theme.

Minimum to Earn Credit

  • Primary Texts: At least 10 texts that clearly connect to your chosen theme.

    • Acceptable: short stories, plays, poems, songs, essays, films, TV episodes, video games, excerpts from novels, etc.

    • Avoid: biographies, plot summaries, encyclopedias, or generic websites.

    • Multiple authors are required; you may use no more than 3 texts by the same author.

  • Length: At least 5 full pages of original editorial writing (not counting cover, table of contents, or the texts themselves).

  • Voice: Editorial and academic in tone; written in third person (you may refer to yourself as “the editor” if needed).

Required Components

  1. Cover / Title Page

    • Original anthology title + your name as “Editor.”

  2. Table of Contents (1–2 pages)

    • List all included works, your Foreword, Preface, and any extras. Page numbers may be invented.

  3. Foreword (Literary Analysis)

    • This is your previously completed Literary Analysis essay repurposed as the critical forward.

    • It introduces and analyzes the one course text you are required to include.

    • Explains how this text connects to your anthology’s theme.

  4. Preface / Introduction (minimum 3 pages)

    • Draw readers in with the theme and explain why it matters.

    • Present your organizing principle: how do these works fit together?

    • Discuss what each text contributes to the theme, comparing and contrasting where useful.

    • Conclude by persuading readers of the anthology’s value and relevance.

Optional “Extras” (for creativity/extra credit)

Dedication, epilogue, about the editor, images, maps, headnotes, glossary, discussion questions, index, recommendations for further reading.
