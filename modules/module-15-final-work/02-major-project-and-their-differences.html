Major Projects Overview

Major Projects

This semester, you will have two large projects to complete. One is a literary analysis paper. The other is to create an anthology. These may use the same theme and one of the same texts. 

Literary Analysis

What is a literary analysis? The word analysis means to break something into its parts. For an analysis then, you examine the parts of a piece of literature through a specific lens. For example, in this class, we look at Medea through the lens of gender roles. To write an analysis, you would then only choose the parts of the story that fit that theme and discuss them. 

This assignment asks you to pick a theme (like gender roles in the example above). We explore a different theme nearly every single week in this class, but you can pick your own if you want. Then you must pick a text we have read in this class to examine. Search that text for events or actions or quotes that fit that theme. As you can imagine, to write a literary analysis, you must be really familiar with the text. 

In your paper, use specific examples and quotations from your text to prove to me that your text is about the theme you have chosen. 

The last part of this assignment asks you to then connect your text and your theme to a contemporary issue. For example, if you are discussing gender roles in Madea, how does that apply to the world today? Are we still facing the same issues? 

You must use at least one outside scholarly source in this paper to back up what you are saying. 

The full directions for this assignment, example assignments, and additional information can be found on the Literary Analysis Essay page.

Anthology 

What is an anthology? An anthology is a book that contains multiple works written by multiple different authors. Typically, these are organized by theme. For this assignment, you may want to use the same theme you used for the literary analysis. 

Now pretend you are making a book (anthology) based on this theme. What texts would you include? Obviously, you would include the text from your literary analysis, but what others? Would you include other texts from this class? Then define the term "text" loosely; we aren't really making a book; we are pretending, so we can do cool things like put songs or video games or movies in our anthology. What outside texts fit your theme? You need ten texts for this assignment.

NOTE: This is NOT an essay.

First, you need to create a title page. What is the name of your anthology? Who wrote it? (you!)

Second, create a table of contents (just like you'd see in the front of any anthology!). This page needs to list your ten texts and shows how they're organized. 

Third, write the preface. This should be three pages, and it should show how your ten texts relate to your theme and why you organized them the way you did.

The full prompt for this assignment, examples, and more information can be found on the Anthology Project page.
