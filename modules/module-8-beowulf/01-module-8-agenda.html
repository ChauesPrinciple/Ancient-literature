Learning Objectives

Upon completion of this module, students will be able to:

  1. MO8.1 Investigate the connection between Societal Superstructures and the feeling of betrayal, according to Dr. Shay.
  2. MO8.2 Identify the role of leadership and generosity in the wellbeing of the warrior.
  3. MO8.3 Explain the relationship between gift giving and the role of leadership.
  4. MO8.4 Evaluate student responses to the Chapter Theory discussion (opened in week one) by responding to other students.

Learning Activities

  1. Read: Historical background, summary, and lecture for Beowulf.
  2. Read: Beowulf. Answer the question at the end of the text.

  3. This week, please view the following multimedia materials:

    • This video by Overly Sarcastic Productions provides a summary and background analysis of the text.

    • This video provides an alternative summary and analysis of the text.
    1. Cædmon's Hymn (Medieval Tales in Performance) – an example of Old English
    2. What is a Kenning? (A Lovely Jaunt)
    3. Deep Drive: The English Language
      1. Anglo-Saxon (1), and
      2. Norman Conquest (2) 
  4. Complete: This week, please make sure you complete the Literary Quick Take for this week's reading, Beowulf, and ensure that you have responded to other students for the Chapter Two Theory Discussion.

Reflect & Discuss

Engage with the text and your peers using the reading questions above.
